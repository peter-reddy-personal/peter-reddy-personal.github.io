Zwift Ladder route picker

What this tool does

The Zwift Ladder Route Picker helps choose the best race route based on the strengths of your team and the likely riders who will be racing with you. Enter your CLS riders and your expected opponents, set how likely each rider is to race, and the tool uses the input vELO2 scores to analyse every Zwift route to show where your team has the biggest advantage.

To input vELO2 scores you can type them in manually, or go to a rider's profile on ZwiftRacing.app, and copy-paste their most recent vELO2 scores (paste into the SPR box and they should fill across).

vELO2 explained ▼

vELO2 scores every rider and route across six performance factors: sprint, punch, climb, TT, pursuit, and endurance. The route weighting is split approx 50% between sprint, punch and climb based on the finish profile of the route, and approx 50% between pursuit and endurance based on its length/overall difficulty. TT is not used in team racing.

A flat finish such as Glasgow Crit Circuit emphasises sprint and punch, while a 5 minute wall finish like Petite Douleur is entirely weighted toward climbing. Pursuit and endurance measure how well a rider survives the demands of shorter v. longer races.

A rider’s vELO2 score for a route is calculated by multiplying their individual factor strengths by the importance (weight) of each factor for that route. The tool then ranks routes by the average vELO2 advantage between the two teams, selecting the routes where each team has the strongest statistical edge.

What does Lik% mean? ▼

Lik% is the percentage likelihood that a rider will race. It lets you include riders who are uncertain or unlikely to race without removing them entirely. A rider with 50% Lik contributes half their vELO2 score to the team average.

You can make up realistic values based on your own guesses, or set each rider to 100% (or use any non-zero % for all riders) to not think about it.
