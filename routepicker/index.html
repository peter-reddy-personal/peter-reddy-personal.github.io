Zwift Ladder route picker

What this tool does

The Zwift Ladder Route Picker helps choose the best race route based on the strengths of your team and the potential riders racing against you. Enter your opponents, remove riders not racing, and the tool uses the input vELO2 scores to analyse every Zwift route to show where your team has the biggest advantage.

Team rosters are taken from a cached version of the Club Ladder site, and may miss newly added or removed riders (within the last few days).

vELO2 (current) and power data (90d best) is input automatically from ZwiftRacing.app.

vELO2 explained ▼

vELO2 scores every rider and route across six performance factors: sprint, punch, climb, TT, pursuit, and endurance. The route weighting is split approx 50% between sprint, punch and climb based on the finish profile of the route, and approx 50% between pursuit and endurance based on its length/overall difficulty. TT is not used in team racing.

A flat finish such as Glasgow Crit Circuit emphasises sprint and punch, while a 5 minute wall finish like Petite Douleur is entirely weighted toward climbing. Pursuit and endurance measure how well a rider survives the demands of shorter v. longer races. There is a list of all the route weightings here.

A rider’s vELO2 score for a route is calculated by multiplying their individual factor strengths by the importance (weight) of each factor for that route. The tool then ranks routes by the average vELO2 advantage between the two teams, selecting the routes where each team has the strongest statistical edge.
